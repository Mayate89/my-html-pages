Loneliness
孤独

Everybody feels lonely // from time to time. //
每个人都会时不时地感到孤独。//

When we have no one to sit next to at lunch, // When we move to a new city // or when nobody has time for us at the weekend.
当我们午餐时没有人坐在旁边时，// 当我们搬到一个新城市时 // 或者当周末没有人有时间陪伴我们时。

But over the last few decades, // this occasional feeling has become chronic for millions.
但在过去的几十年里 ，// 这种偶尔的感觉已经成为数百万人的慢性病。

In the UK, // 60% of 18 to 34 year olds say they often feel lonely.
在英国，// 60% 的 18 至 34 岁人群表示他们经常感到孤独。

In the US, // 46% of the entire population feel lonely regularly.
在美国，// 46% 的人口经常感到孤独。

We are living in the most connected time in human history, // and yet, // an unprecedented number of us feel isolated.
我们生活在人类历史上联系最紧密的时代，// 然而 ，// 我们中空前有更多的人感到孤立 。

Being lonely and being alone // are not the same thing.
孤独和孤独//不是一回事 。

You can be filled by bliss by yourself, // and hate every second surrounded by friends.
你可以自己被幸福所填满 ，// 讨厌被朋友包围的每一秒。

Loneliness is a purely subjective, // individual experience.
孤独是一种纯粹主观的 、//个人的体验。

If you feel lonely, // you are lonely.
如果你感到孤独，// 你很孤独。

A common stereotype is that loneliness only happens to people // who don't know how to talk to people, // or how to behave around others.
一个常见的刻板印象是，孤独只发生在//不知道如何与人交谈，//或如何在他人面前表现的人身上。

But population-based studies have shown // that social skills make practically no difference for adults // when it comes to social connections.
但基于人群的研究表明 ，社交技能对成年人几乎没有影响 //在社交关系方面 。

Loneliness can affect everybody.
孤独会影响每个人 。

Money, // fame, // power, // beauty, // social skills, // a great personality— // nothing can protect you against loneliness, // because it's part of your biology.
金钱，// 名声，// 权力，// 美丽，// 社交技巧，// 伟大的个性 — // 没有什么可以保护你免受孤独，// 因为它是你生物学的一部分。

Loneliness is a bodily function, // like hunger.
孤独是一种身体功能 ，// 就像饥饿一样。

Hunger makes you pay attention to your physical needs; // loneliness makes you pay attention to your social needs.
饥饿让你关注自己的身体需求;孤独让你关注自己的社交需求。

Your body cares about your social needs, // because millions of years ago, // it was a great indicator of how likely you were to survive.
你的身体关心你的社交需求，// 因为在数百万年前，// 这是你生存可能性的一个很好的指标 。

Natural selection rewarded our ancestors for collaboration, // and for forming connections with each other.
自然选择奖励了我们的祖先的合作，// 以及彼此之间建立联系。

Our brains grew // and became more and more fine-tuned to recognize what others thought and felt, // and to form and sustain social bonds.
我们的大脑成长//并变得越来越精细 ，能够识别他人的想法和感受，//并形成和维持社会纽带 。

Being social became part of our biology.
社交成为我们生物学的一部分。

You were born into groups of 50 to 150 people, // which you usually stayed with // for the rest of your life.
你出生在 50 到 150 人的群体中，// 你通常和他们在一起 // 度过你的余生 。

Getting enough calories, // staying safe and warm, // or caring for offspring // was practically impossible alone.
获得足够的卡路里，//保持安全和温暖，//或照顾后代//单独是不可能的 。

Being together meant survival, // being alone meant death.
在一起意味着生存 ，// 独处意味着死亡 。

So it was crucial that you got along with others.
所以你必须和别人相处得好 。

For your ancestors, // the most dangerous threat to survival // was not being eaten by a lion, // but not getting the social vibe of your group // and being excluded.
对于你的祖先来说，// 生存最危险的威胁 // 没有被狮子吃掉，// 但没有得到你群体的社交氛围 // 并被排除在外 。

To avoid that, // your body came up with "social pain".
为了避免这种情况 ，// 你的身体想出了 “社交痛苦”。

Pain of this kind // is an evolutionary adaptation to rejection.
这种痛苦//是对拒绝的进化适应 。

A sort of early warning system // to make sure you stop behaviour that would isolate you.
一种预警系统 // 以确保您停止会孤立您的行为。

Your ancestors who experienced rejection as more painful // were more likely to change their behaviour when they got rejected, // and thus stayed in the tribe, // while those who did not got kicked out // and most likely died.
那些经历过被拒绝更痛苦的祖先，// 在被拒绝后更可能改变行为，// 因此留在部落，// 而那些没有被排斥的人则被赶出 // 很可能死亡。

That's why rejections hurt, // and even more so, // why loneliness is so painful.
这就是为什么被拒绝会让人痛苦，// 更甚的是，// 为什么孤独如此痛苦 。

These mechanisms for keeping us connected // worked great for most of our history, // until humans began building a new world for themselves.
这些保持我们联系的机制在我们大部分历史时期都运作良好 ， 直到人类开始为自己建造一个新世界。

The loneliness epidemic we see today // really only started in the late Renaissance.
我们今天看到的孤独流行病其实是在文艺复兴晚期才开始的。

Western culture began to focus on the individual.
西方文化开始关注个人 。

Intellectuals moved away from the collectivism of the Middle Ages, // while the young Protestant theology stressed individual responsibility.
知识分子逐渐远离了中世纪的集体主义 ，// 而年轻的新教神学则强调个人责任 。

This trend accelerated during the Industrial Revolution.
这一趋势在工业革命期间加速。

People left their villages and fields to enter factories.
人们离开村庄和田地进入工厂。

Communities that had existed for hundreds of years // began to dissolve, // while cities grew.
存在了数百年的社区开始解体 ，城市不断发展。

As our world rapidly became modern, // this trend sped up more and more.
随着世界迅速现代化，这一趋势也越来越快 。

Today, // we move vast distances for new jobs, // love, // and education, // and leave our social net behind.
如今，// 我们为了新工作、爱情、// 和教育而远离千里，// 离开了我们的社交网络 。

We meet fewer people in person, // and we meet them less often than in the past.
我们见面的人比以前少，// 也比以前少。

In the US, // the mean number of close friends dropped from 3 in 1985 // to 2 in 2011.
在美国，// 亲密朋友的平均人数从 1985 年的 3 人///下降到 2011 年的 2 人。

Most people stumble into chronic loneliness by accident.
大多数人都是偶然陷入慢性孤独。

You reach adulthood and become busy with work, // university, // romance, // kids, // and Netflix.
你到了成年，开始忙于工作、// 大学、// 恋爱、// 孩子、// 和 Netflix。

There's just not enough time.
时间根本不够。

The most convenient and easy thing to sacrifice // is time with friends.
最方便且最容易牺牲的东西是和朋友相处的时间。

Until you wake up one day // and you realise that you feel isolated, // that you yearn for close relationships.
直到有一天你醒来 // 你意识到自己感到孤立，// 渴望亲密的关系。

But it's hard to find close relationships as adults, // and so, // loneliness can become chronic.
但成年后很难找到亲密关系，// 因此，// 孤独可能会变成慢性。

While humans feel pretty great about things like iPhones and spaceships, // our bodies and minds are fundamentally the same they were 50,000 years ago.
虽然人类对 iPhone 和飞船等东西感觉很好，但// 我们的身体和心智本质上和五万年前是一样的。

We are still biologically fine-tuned to being with each other.
我们仍然在生物学上精准地与彼此相处。

large scale studies have shown // that the stress that comes from chronic loneliness // is among the most unhealthy things we can experience as humans.
大规模研究表明 ，长期孤独带来的压力是我们作为人类可能经历的最不健康的事情之一。

It makes you age quicker, // it makes cancer deadlier, // Alzheimer's advance faster, // your immune systems weaker.
它让你衰老得更快，// 它让癌症更致命，// 阿尔茨海默病进展得更快，// 你的免疫系统更弱。

Loneliness is twice as deadly as obesity // and as deadly as smoking a pack of ciggarettes a day.
孤独的致命性是肥胖的两倍//，也比每天抽一包香烟更致命。

The most dangerous thing about it is that // once it becomes chronic it can become self-sustaining.
最危险的是， 一旦变成慢性病，可能会自我维持 。

Physical and social pain use common mechanisms in your brain.
身体和社会疼痛使用大脑中常见的机制。

Both feel like a threat // and so social pain leads to immediate and defensive behaviour // when it's inflicted on you.
两者都感觉像威胁//因此社交痛苦会在施加在你身上时立即表现出防御性的行为//

when loneliness becomes chronic // your brain goes into self preservation mode.
当孤独变成慢性时 // 你的大脑会进入自我保护模式 。

it starts to see danger and hostility everywhere. // but that's not all.
它开始到处看到危险和敌意。 但这还不是全部 。

Some studies found that when you're lonely // your brain is much more receptive and alert to social signals, // while at the same time // it gets worse at interpreting them correctly.
一些研究发现，当你感到孤独时，// 你的大脑对社交信号的接受和警觉性会大大提高，// 但同时 // 它在正确解读它们方面会变得更差。

You pay more attention to others // but you understand them less.
你会更关注别人 // 但你理解他们却更少 。

The part of you brain that recognises faces // gets out of tune // and becomes more likely to categorise neutral faces as hostile, // which makes it distrustful of others.
你大脑中识别面孔的部分会走调 ，// 并更容易将中立面孔归类为敌对 ，// 这使它对他人产生不信任 。

Loneliness makes you assume the worst about others' intentions towards you.
孤独让你对别人对你的意图抱有最坏的假设 。

Because of this perceived hostile world, // you can become more self-centred to protect yourself, // which can make you appear more cold, // unfriendly // and socially awkward than you really are.
正因为这种被认为敌对的世界，// 你可能会变得更自我中心以保护自己，// 这会让你看起来比实际更冷漠、更不友好、更社交尴尬 。

If loneliness has become a strong presence in your life, // the first thing you can do is to try to recognise the vicious cycle you may be trapped in.
如果孤独已经成为你生活中强烈的存在，// 你首先可以做的就是试着识别你可能陷入的恶性循环 。

It usually goes something like this: // an initial feeling of isolation leads to feelings of tension and sadness, // which makes you focus your attention selectively on negative interactions with others.
通常大致是这样的：// 最初的孤立感会引发紧张和悲伤的感觉，// 这会让你选择性地关注与他人的负面互动。

This makes your thoughts about yourself and others more negative, // which then changes your behaviour.
这会让你对自己和他人的想法变得更负面，// 进而改变你的行为。

You begin to avoid social interaction, // which leads to more feelings of isolation.
你开始避免社交互动，// 这导致更多的孤立感。

This cycle becomes more severe and harder to escape // each time loneliness make you sit far away from others in class, // not answer the phone when friends call, // decline invitations // until the invitations stop.
每当孤独让你在课堂上远离他人，// 不接朋友电话，// 拒绝邀请 // 直到邀请停止，这个循环就变得更严重、更难逃脱。

Each and every one of us has a story about ourselves // and if your story becomes that people exclude you, // others pick up on that // and so the outside world can become the way you feel about it.
我们每个人都有自己的故事 // 如果你的故事变成了别人排斥你，// 其他人会察觉到 // 这样外面的世界就会变成你对它的感受。

This is often a slow creeping process that takes years // and can end in depression // and a mental state that can prevent connections // even if you yearn for them.
这通常是一个缓慢的过程 ，需要数年时间 // 最终可能导致抑郁 // 以及一种即使你渴望的心理状态，也可能阻碍连接。

The first thing you can do to escape it // is to accept that loneliness is a totally normal feeling // and nothing to be ashamed of.
你能做的第一件事就是接受孤独是一种完全正常的感觉，没什么好羞愧的。

Literally everybody feels lonely at some point in their life, // it's a universal human experience.
每个人一生中都会感到孤独，// 这是人类普遍的经历 。

You can't eliminate or ignore a feeling until it goes away magically, // but you can accept that you feel it // and get rid of its cause.
你无法消除或忽视一种感觉，直到它神奇地消失 ，// 但你可以接受自己有这种感觉 // 并摆脱它的原因 。

you can self examine what you focus your attention on // and check if you are selectively concentrating on negative things.
你可以自我审视你关注的方向 //，检查自己是否选择性地专注于负面事物。

was this interaction with a colleague really negative? // or was it really mutual or even positive?
这次和同事的互动真的很负面吗？还是说这真的是双方的 ，甚至是积极的？

what was the actual content of an interaction? // what did the other person say?
互动的实际内容是什么？对方说了什么？

did they say something bad // or did you add extra meaning to their words?
他们说了什么难听的话吗 // 还是你给他们的话加了额外的含义 ？

maybe another person was not really reacting negatively // but just short on time.
也许另一个人其实并没有真正负面反应 // 只是时间紧迫 。

then there are your thoughts about the world.
还有你对世界的看法。

are you assuming the worst about others' intentions?
你是不是对别人的意图抱有最坏的假设？

do you enter a social situation and have already decided how it will go?
你是否进入了一个社交场合，并且已经决定了事情的发展方向？

do you assume others don't want you around?
你以为别人不想要你吗？

are you trying to avoid being hurt // and not risking opening up?
你是在努力避免受伤，不愿敞开心扉吗？

and if so, // can you try to give others the benefit of the doubt?
如果是这样 ，// 你能不能试着给别人一些信任的余地？

can you just assume that they're not against you?
你能不能就这么认为他们不是针对你？

can you risk being open and vulnerable again?
你能冒险再次敞开心扉、展现脆弱吗？

And lastly // your behaviour,
最后 // 你的行为，

are you avoiding opportunities to be around others?
你是否在回避与他人相处的机会？

are you looking of opportunites to decline invitations? // or are you pushing away others' preemptively to protect yourself?
你是在寻找拒绝邀请的机会吗？还是你为了保护自己而提前疏远别人的关系？

are you acting as if you are getting attacked?
你是不是表现得像被攻击了一样？

are you really looking for new connections // or have you become complacent with your situation?
你真的在寻找新的人脉吗？还是已经对现状感到自满？

of course // every person in each situation is unique and different. // and just introspection alone might not be enough.
当然 ，// 每个人在每种情况下都是独一无二且不同的。 仅靠内省可能还不够。

if you feel unable to solve your situation alone by yourself, // please try to reach out and get professional help. // It's not a sign of weakness // but of courage.
如果你觉得自己无法独自解决这个问题，// 请尝试寻求专业帮助。// 这不是软弱的表现 // 而是勇气的表现。

However we look at loneliness // as a purely individual problem that needs solving to create happiness // or as a public health crisis, // it is something that deserves more attention.
然而，我们通常把孤独看作是一个需要解决以创造幸福的个人问题，或者把它看作公共卫生危机，// 它确实值得更多关注 。

Humans have built a world that is nothing short of amazing, // and yet not of the shiny things we have made // is able to satisfy or substitute // our fundamental and biological need for connection.
人类创造了一个令人惊叹的世界，// 但我们创造的那些闪亮的东西// 无法满足或替代我们对连接的基本且生物的需求。

Most animals get what they need from their physical sourroundings, // we get what we need from each other // and we need to build our artificial human world based on that.
大多数动物从它们的物理环境中获得所需的东西，// 我们彼此之间得到所需的 // 然后我们需要基于这些来构建我们的人工人类世界。
