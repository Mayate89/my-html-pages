主题5: 习语与俗语 (Idioms & Sayings) - 续

性质 (Nature) 语料块 (Chunk) 注释与提示 (Annotation & Note) 第一人称例句 (1st-Person Example)
[Phrase] spin a bit out of here 从这里脱身/溜走 (Note: *俚语*) This party is getting boring; I think it's time for me to spin a bit out of here.
[Phrase] steal someone’s thunder 抢某人的风头 I was about to announce my good news, but my sister stole my thunder by announcing her engagement.
[Phrase] stick to your guns 坚持自己的立场 Even though everyone disagreed with me, I decided to stick to my guns.
[Phrase] Stop with the lies 别再说谎了 I know what really happened, so please just stop with the lies.
[Phrase] sweep it under the rug / sweep things under the rug 掩盖问题, 隐瞒 I don't think we should just sweep this issue under the rug; we need to address it.
[Phrase] swing and a miss (棒球)挥棒落空, 尝试但失败了 I tried to make a joke to lighten the mood, but it was a total swing and a miss.
[Phrase] take a back seat 退居次要位置 I think it's time for me to take a back seat and let the younger generation lead.
[Phrase] take a rain check 改天再说, 以后再约 I'm too tired to go out tonight, can I take a rain check?
[Phrase] take it down a notch 降低一点程度, 冷静点 I understand you're angry, but you need to take it down a notch.
[Phrase] take it with a pinch of salt / take it with a grain of salt 持保留/怀疑态度, 不可全信 He tends to exaggerate, so I'd take anything he says with a grain of salt.
[Phrase] take the bull by the horns 当机立断, 大胆面对困难 I decided to take the bull by the horns and confront my boss about the issue.
[Phrase] take the win 接受胜利, 算我赢了 I know it wasn't a fair game, but I'll take the win.
[Phrase] the ball is in your court 该你做决定了, 球在你那边了 I've done everything I can to help. Now, the ball is in your court.
[Phrase] the best of both worlds 两全其美 Working from home gives me the best of both worlds: I can have a career and spend more time with my family.
[Phrase] the icing on the cake 锦上添花 (有时也用于反语, 雪上加霜) I was so happy to get the promotion, and the company car was the icing on the cake.
[Phrase] the last straw / the straw that broke the camel’s back 最后一根稻草, 忍无可忍的最后一件事 I was already having a bad day, and his rude comment was the last straw.
[Phrase] throw in the towel 认输, 放弃 After trying for hours to fix the computer, I finally threw in the towel and called a technician.
[Phrase] turn a blind eye 视而不见, 睁一只眼闭一只眼 I know he's breaking the rules, but for now, I'm going to turn a blind eye.
[Phrase] turn a deaf ear 充耳不闻 I tried to give him advice, but he turned a deaf ear to everything I said.
[Phrase] turn over a new leaf 翻开新的一页, 改过自新 After that incident, I decided it was time to turn over a new leaf.
[Phrase] under the radar 不引人注意, 低调 I'm trying to stay under the radar at work while I look for a new job.
[Phrase] under the weather 身体不舒服 I'm feeling a bit under the weather today, so I think I'll stay home.
[Phrase] under your nose 就在你眼皮底下 I can't believe the solution was right under my nose the whole time.
[Phrase] up in arms 非常愤怒, 准备抗争 The local community is up in arms about the plan to build a new highway here.
[Phrase] up in the air 悬而未决 My travel plans are still up in the air; I haven't booked anything yet.
[Phrase] walk on eggshells 小心翼翼, 如履薄冰 Whenever my boss is in a bad mood, I feel like I have to walk on eggshells around her.
[Phrase] walk on thin ice 如履薄冰, 处境危险 After being late three times this week, I know I'm walking on thin ice with my manager.
[Phrase] walk the talk 言行一致 I respect leaders who don't just talk about values but actually walk the talk.
[Phrase] water under the bridge 已成往事, 不念旧恶 We had our disagreements in the past, but that's all water under the bridge now.
[Phrase] when pigs fly 除非太阳从西边出来, 绝不可能 He'll clean his room when pigs fly.
[Phrase] you can’t have your cake and eat it too 鱼与熊掌不可兼得 I know I can't have my cake and eat it too; I have to choose between the high-paying job and having more free time.
[Phrase] you can’t judge a book by its cover 人不可貌相, 不能以貌取人 He might look intimidating, but you can't judge a book by its cover; he's actually very kind.
