[Phrase] don’t let … get in your head 别让…影响你的心态/让你胡思乱想 I know the interview was tough, but don't let it get in your head. You did your best.
[Phrase] Get over yourself. 别自以为是了 (Note: *口语化*, 用于让某人别太把自己当回事。) I know you think you're the best player, but get over yourself. It's a team game.
[Phrase] He is in a feud with her. 他跟她有宿怨/长期不和 I'm trying to organize a dinner, but it's difficult because he is in a feud with her.
[Phrase] I feel the odd man out. 我感觉自己格格不入 (Note: `odd one out` 也可以。) When all my friends started talking about their kids, I felt like the odd man out.
[Phrase] If it’s any consolation, … 如果这能给你一点安慰的话… I know you lost the match, but if it's any consolation, you played incredibly well.
[Phrase] I’ll see you home. 我送你回家 (Note: 指护送。) It's getting late. Don't worry, I'll see you home safely.
[Phrase] You could’ve at least included me in this decision. 你至少应该让我也参与这个决定 I feel hurt that you sold our car without discussing it with me. You could've at least included me in this decision.
[Phrase] You’ve got nothing to worry about. 你没什么好担心的 I've already taken care of everything, so you've got nothing to worry about.
[Action/Verb] back down (on ...) (在...上)让步, 放弃立场 I argued for my idea, but I had to back down when my boss disagreed.
[Action/Verb] Bail on me 放我鸽子 I can't believe he bailed on me for our movie night again.
[Action/Verb] Bail you out 帮你摆脱困境 (尤指经济上) I'm in a bit of financial trouble, and I was hoping my parents could bail me out.
[Action/Verb] bump into each other 不期而遇, 碰见 I can't believe we bumped into each other in a different country!
[Action/Verb] butt heads 发生冲突, 争执 I'm trying to work with him, but we butt heads on almost every issue.
[Action/Verb] chime in, weigh in 插话, 发表意见 (Note: `chime in`更随意, `weigh in`稍正式。) I was about to make a decision when my colleague chimed in with a great suggestion.
[Action/Verb] come around (最终)改变主意, 同意 I didn't like the idea at first, but after hearing your arguments, I'm starting to come around.
[Action/Verb] crack sb up 把(某人)逗笑 His jokes are so silly, but they always crack me up.
[Action/Verb] dish out compliments 分发赞美, 恭维 I'm not the type of person who just dishes out compliments unless I really mean them.

[Action/Verb] put in my two cents 发表我的浅见 If I can put in my two cents, I think we should reconsider the entire plan.

[Action/Verb] screw you over on ... 在...上坑你/欺骗你 I can't believe he tried to screw me over on the final price.
[Action/Verb] sign off on 正式批准, 签字同意 I'm just waiting for my manager to sign off on my vacation request.
[Action/Verb] sulk around 到处生闷气 After he lost the game, I saw him sulking around the house all day.
[Action/Verb] take... seriously 认真对待... I hope you take my advice seriously; it could really help you.
[Action/Verb] win ... over 赢得...的支持/好感 I tried my best to win her over with my sense of humor.

[Connector] For the record 我得郑重声明一下... (Note: 用于强调某件事，希望对方记住。) For the record, I was never in favor of this plan.

[Connector] Here’s the thing 问题是..., 关键是... (Note: *口语化*, 用于引出核心问题或观点。) I'd love to help you move. Here's the thing, I have a bad back.

[Connector] in a bid to (do...) 为了(做...) I started waking up earlier in a bid to be more productive.
[Connector] in an attempt to 试图... I called him in an attempt to clear up the misunderstanding.
[Connector] In case you didn’t notice……. 以防你没注意到... (Note: *常带讽刺意味*) In case you didn't notice, I've been waiting here for an hour.
[Connector] In fairness 公平地说... He made a big mistake, but in fairness, he's very young and inexperienced.
[Connector] in some respects 在某些方面 In some respects, I agree with your point of view.
[Connector] In the spirit of 本着...的精神 In the spirit of cooperation, I'm willing to compromise on this issue.
[Connector] in terms of 在...方面 In terms of salary, the new job offer is great, but the commute is very long.
[Connector] It goes without saying that… 不言而喻... It goes without saying that I'll be there to support you on your big day.

[Connector] Just to be clear 只是为了说清楚 Just to be clear, I am not agreeing to work on weekends.
[Connector] last time I checked 据我所知, 我上次确认的时候 (Note: *常带讽刺或强调意味*) Last time I checked, I was the one in charge here.
[Connector] more often than not 多半, 往往 More often than not, I find that a simple solution is the best one.
[Connector] Now that I think about it 现在回想起来... Now that I think about it, I think I've met him before.
[Connector] off the record 私下说, 不公开的 Off the record, I think this whole project is a bad idea.
[Connector] on the flip side 从另一方面来说... (Note: 用于引出相反或不同的观点。) The work is hard, but on the flip side, the pay is excellent.
[Connector] Once in a while 偶尔, 间或 I'm not a big movie-goer, but I enjoy watching a film once in a while.

[Connector] with all due respect 恕我直言... (Note: *礼貌地表达反对意见*的前奏。) With all due respect, professor, I believe there's another way to interpret the data.
[Connector] You know what I think it is? 你知道我认为是什么吗? I can't figure out why the machine is broken. You know what I think it is? The power supply.
